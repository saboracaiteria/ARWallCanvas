# **Augmented Graffiti** — Manifesto of Digital Mark-making

## The Movement

**Augmented Graffiti** is an aesthetic and functional movement born at the intersection of street art, augmented reality, and architectural intervention. It treats physical walls not as surfaces to be permanently altered, but as temporal canvases that exist simultaneously in the real and the virtual. The work is viewed through a lens — a phone, AR glasses, or VR headset — but the presence of the artist's hand, the weight of the gesture, the grain of the digital spray, must feel as real as aerosol on brick.

## Space, Form, and Material

The philosophy rejects the flat, floating images of conventional AR. Every mark must be **anchored to physical depth** — contouring to the wall's bumps, receding into corners, respecting windows and pipes. The form language is part architectural blueprint, part wildstyle graffiti: sharp perspective lines intersect organic spray bursts. Materials are digital simulations of real-world substances — cement dust, oxidized metal, dried ink, neon gas. Nothing is purely virtual; everything carries the memory of a physical reference.

## Color and Light

The palette is **site-responsive**: the camera feed's ambient color temperature informs the overlay hues. Warm sodium lamps shift the digital palette toward amber and rust; cold daylight brings cyan and steel. Augmented Graffiti never outshines its environment — it harmonizes, sometimes contrasting only in texture, not luminance. The result is a ghostly, embedded presence that appears to have always been there, waiting to be discovered.

## Scale, Rhythm, and Composition

Compositions are built for **architectural scale** — whole walls, building sides, tunnel arches. The rhythm is the artist's hand moving through 3D space: long sweeping lines, rapid staccato dots, deliberate pauses. The digital brush respects the same physics as a real one — inertia, pressure, velocity. Each stroke is a record of time and motion, not a parametric output.

## The Craftsmanship Imperative

This philosophy demands that every pixel of the projected image be placed with the patience of a master fresco painter. No auto-fill, no lazy shading, no pre-baked textures. The digital spray can must be held and wielded as if it were real — the pressure, the distance from the wall, the angle of the wrist. The result must scream **countless hours of deliberate practice** — the kind of effort that separates a tagger from a muralist. When a user finishes a piece inside the app, it should look like it took days, because the care in every stroke is visible.

## Minimal Text, Maximal Expression

Text in Augmented Graffiti is rare and intentional — a single word floating in perspective, a date carved into a corner, a signature rendered as a holographic watermark. Visual information carries everything: line weight tells the story of speed, color saturation records the mood, layering reveals the process. The viewer reads the art with their eyes, not with a caption.
